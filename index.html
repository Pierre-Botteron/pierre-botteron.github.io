My PhD subject

Here are some keywords describing my subject:

  • Nonlocal games (e.g. CHSH game),
  • Nonlocal boxes (e.g. PR box),
  • Post-quantum strategies,
  • Communication complexity (it can be whether "trivial" or "non-trivial"),
There is a strong belief in the computer science community that "communication complexity" (as defined in [Yao79]) has to be non-trivial if it describes a scenario that is plausible in Nature, which we rephraase in: ressources that exist in Nature are seemingly "non-collapsing". Then the open question we consider is: which post-quantum correlations are "collapsing"? It would yield the intuition that those post-quantum correlations are unlikely to exist in Nature.

My supervisors

The superving team is composed in three complementary parts in Quantun Information Theory:
  • Anne Broadbent (University of Ottawa), expert in quantum cryptography, nonlocal, quantum nonlocality and quantum complexity theory.
  • Ion Nechita (CNRS, University of Toulouse), expert in random matrix theory, with a recent focus on the theory of random tensors, and in the theory of quantum entanglement.
  • Clément Pellegrini (University of Toulouse), expert on stochastic aspects of quantum mechanics, with a focus on the theory of quantum trajectories and open quantum systems.
